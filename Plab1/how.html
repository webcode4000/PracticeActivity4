Foul Shots, also called "free throws," are used in basketball to penalize players for committing fouls, especially while making shots for points. When a player is fouled while trying to score, they are awarded free throws as a way of compensating for the unfair advantage gained by the fouling player.

So why the Foul Shot?

Foul shots are crucial in basketball for several reasons:

  • Promotes Fair Play: By penalizing fouls, the foul shot helps players stick to the rules and promotes fair play.

  • Increases Scoring Opportunities: Free throws provide teams with additional chances to score points, which can be critical in close games.

  • Strategic Element: Coaches often develop strategies around free throw shooting, including deciding when to foul intentionally to stop the clock or to put poor free throw shooters on the line. This may not seem ethical, but it has been seen consistently among collegiate and professional teams.

Steps to Make a Foul Shot

  1. Stand behind the free-throw line.
  2. Hold the ball with your shooting hand under the ball and your guide hand on the side.
  3. Focus on the basket and take a deep breath.
  4. Bend your knees slightly and keep your feet shoulder-width apart.
  5. As you rise, extend your shooting arm and release the ball at the peak of your jump.
